1. Make sure you're eligible to work in Canada

Vancouver is historically friendly to immigrants, and getting a visa in Canada is relatively easy compared to many countries in the world. For many immigration hopefuls, Express Entry is an option - there are truly few barriers for working in Canada beyond finding employment. If you’d like to check if you qualify for express entry, you can check out your eligibility at Canada's Immigration services website. Knowing your eligibility up front can save you time on your job search in case you don’t qualify.



2.Consider the type of available jobs

Vancouver is one of Canada's tech hubs, and as such the city boasts plenty of jobs in the technology sector. Other big industries in the city include media, finance and tourism, with the city boasting a growing startup scene.


Outside the core industries, there’s still a pretty wide range of industries that are booming. Knowing which ones are the biggest can help you decide whether Vancouver is the right city for you; it’s important to research your industry’s prevalence as you begin your job search.



3. Look online

When looking for jobs, the Internet provides a wide range of opportunities across many sectors and is typically the best place to start. Some of the most popular sites to search for jobs in Vancouver include

  • Indeed.ca
  • BCjobs.ca
  • Monster.ca
  • Craigslist
  • Google

It's also a good idea to use social media websites like LinkedIn.

4. Networking

A strong professional network is one of the most important things you can have in a job search anywhere, and it’s massively helpful for finding a job in Vancouver. If you have time to visit the city before or during your job search, attending professional events is one of the best things you can do to get a leg up on your competitors.

  • One of the best ways to network is using websites like meetup.com.

5. Make sure your CV and cover letter are up to date.

There are a ton of jobs available in Vancouver, but it’s also necessary to keep in mind that there are are many professionals competing for them. If you want to be selected for the job, you’re going to need to have a CV that really stands out.
If you need general tips for resume writing or a place to get started, try Monster's Resume Writing Tips as well as CareerPerfect's Expert CV Advice. Even if you’re an excellent writer, it can be a good idea to have your resume spruced up by real HR professionals.
